Control Theories and Their Applications in Biology

The laboratory research of Kai Song’s group is concerned with developing and using control theories and technologies in biological fields. The research spans the multiple disciplines of biostatistics, in silico synthetic biology, systems biology, etc. In order to better understand cellular life and how diseases develop, the primary objectives are to: a) identify functional parts of DNA sequences, b) model, design and analyze gene regulatory networks, and, c) utilize microarray data for signature research, survival analysis, and personalized treatment, etc. The additional objectives of process control, however, are still within our research interests.
